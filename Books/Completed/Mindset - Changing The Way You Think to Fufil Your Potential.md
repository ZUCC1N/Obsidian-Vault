## 1.    THE MINDSETS
when presenting children with 2 series of puzzles, the first easy and the second set harder, they seemingly all embraced the challenge of the second set. this goes against what we really expect, they are openly loving the failure instead of the act of coping or not coping. this could come down to how children are sponges, they don't see *failure* but only *learning*.
this really flies against deterministic thinking, you simply are capable or not. kids haven't been presented with that idea yet. this, is the difference in mindset.
### Why Do People Differ?
since the start of written history people have always differed. we think differently, act differently and thus fared differently. this means it was destined for someone to eventually ask why, why some people might be smarter, some moral, and is that intrinsic to each of us. thus began debate from both sides of intrinsic, aka eugenics, or extrinsic. extrinsically we have difference in people's culture, background, training and acquisition of knowledge. the inventor of the IQ test, touted as a measure of raw intrinsic intelligence actually prescribed to this himself, believing the test could help identify children that require more assistance in order to *change* their mindset:
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
A few modern philosophers . . . assert that an individual’s intelligence is a fixed quantity, a quantity which cannot be increased. We must protest and react against this brutal pessimism. . . . With practice, training, and above all, method, we manage to increase our attention, our memory, our judgment and literally to become more intelligent than we were before.<br>
- Alfred Binet (1857-1911)
</i></p>
today, we think its a little bit of both. you have a disposition, but also post conception its down to how environment and genes interact. people have aptitudes but there is no limit to really what one can think or achieve given enough training and effort. engagement can make up for a lack of aptitude, and make the smartest at the start perhaps not the smartest by the end.
### What Does All This Mean for You? The Two Mindsets
but what does this mean to you? research shows that the views you adopt can profoundly affect how you lead your life, determining if you become the person you wish or if you accomplish what you want. believing in intrinsicness -The Fixed Mindset- creates the dilemma and urgency to prove yourself. if you hold a certain character, intelligence, morality, you must continually prove you hold them. take the example of comparing children's intelligence and treating them accordingly. they are conditioned by 1 rule; act smart, not dumb. school becomes less about fun or enjoyment but *proving* you have learnt the right things. it becomes a matter of the haves and haves not, the success or the failure, the accepted or rejected, winners or losers.

on the other hand, you could believe the hand your dealt does have some effect, but isn't the end - The Growing Mindset-. you're now less worried about having a royal flush and more how you can improve that hand with cultivation. you can always change a pair of tens into a royal flush by the end. you won't become a savant genius, you probably can't will yourself into an Einstein of Beethoven, but you also can't rule it out. your true potential is unknown, and always will be. you'll only get there through the grind. who gives a fuck about proving you're smart when you could be toiling to *become*smart. why hide issues instead of overcoming them? why look for yes men when you could look for people to push you? this striving tends to mean the growth mindset allows you to overcome even the greatest obstacles.
### A View from the Two Mindsets
To give a better sense of the 2 mindsets, imagine a young adult having a really bad day. they go to a class that matters to them a lot, but find they got a C+ in a mid term. they feel disappointed. they find on their way home they got a parking ticket. they call their friend to vent their frustration but are sort of brushed off.

what would you think in this situation? what would you feel? what would you do.

the fixed mindset would respond they feel rejected, a failure or an idiot. they see what occurred as a measure of their worth. perhaps the world is against them, everyone hates them, life is unfair. this is an overreaction to what were minor inconveniences. they aren't pessimists or suicidal but, when coping effectively they are as optimistic as people with a growth mindset. but how do they cope? they decide it's not worth putting effort in, stay in bed, get drunk, yell at people, pout, cry, break something. over a non-life ending event, but to the fixed mindset, it may aswell be the end of the world.

when the same situation is given to the growth mindset:
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
“I need to try harder in class, be more careful when parking the car, and wonder if my friend had a bad day.”
</i></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
“The C+ would tell me that I’d have to work a lot harder in the class, but I have the rest of the semester to pull up my grade.”
</i></p>
how would they cope?
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
“I’d start thinking about studying harder (or studying in a different way) for my next test in that class, I’d pay the ticket, and I’d work things out with my best friend the next time we speak.”
</i></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
“I’d look at what was wrong on my exam, resolve to do better, pay my parking ticket, and call my friend to tell her I was upset the day before.”
</i></p>
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
“Work hard on my next paper, speak to the teacher, be more careful where I park or contest the ticket, and find out what’s wrong with my friend.”
</i></p>
the growth mindset doesn't make them not be upset, these still aren't fun experiences. but the difference is they don't take this as the end of the world, only a setback.

### Grow Your Mindset
Which mindset do you have? Read each statement and decide whether you mostly agree with it or disagree with it.

1.   Your intelligence is something very basic about you that you can’t change very much.
2.   You can learn new things, but you can’t really change how intelligent you are.
3.   No matter how much intelligence you have, you can always change it quite a bit.
4.   You can always substantially change how intelligent you are.

Questions 1 and 2 are the fixed-mindset questions. Questions 3 and 4 reflect the growth mindset. You can be a mixture, but most people lean toward one or the other. but these questions can really be applied to any topic, the principle stays the same.

It’s not only your abilities; it’s your personal qualities too.

1.   You are a certain kind of person, and there is not much that can be done to really change that.
2.   No matter what kind of person you are, you can always change substantially.
3.   You can do things differently, but the important parts of who you are can’t really be changed.
4.   You can always change basic things about the kind of person you are.

Did it differ from your intelligence mindset? It can. Your “intelligence mindset” comes into play when situations involve mental ability.

Your “personality mindset” comes into play in situations that involve your personal qualities—for example, how dependable, cooperative, caring, or socially skilled you are. The fixed mindset makes you concerned with how you’ll be judged; the growth mindset makes you concerned with improving.

Here are some more ways to think about mindsets:
 
- Think about someone you know who is steeped in the fixed mindset. Think about how they’re always trying to prove themselves and how they’re supersensitive about being wrong or making mistakes. Did you ever wonder why they were this way? (Are you this way?) Now you can begin to understand why.
- Think about someone you know who is skilled in the growth mindset—someone who understands that important qualities can be cultivated. Think about the ways they confront obstacles. Think about the things they do to stretch themselves. What are some ways you might like to change or stretch yourself?
- Okay, now imagine you’ve decided to learn a new language and you’ve signed up for a class. A few sessions into the course, the instructor calls you to the front of the room and starts throwing questions at you one after another.

Put yourself in a fixed mindset. Your ability is on the line. Can you feel everyone’s eyes on you? Can you see the instructor’s face evaluating you? Feel the tension, feel your ego bristle and waver. What else are you thinking and feeling?

Now put yourself in a growth mindset. You’re a novice—that’s why you’re here. You’re here to learn. The teacher is a resource for learning. Feel the tension leave you; feel your mind open up.

The message is: You can change your mindset.
## 2.    INSIDE THE MINDSETS
in trying to understand why some students were caught up in proving themselves while others just learnt, it was realised that there are 2 meanings to ability, one to be proven and one to be developed. this is the basis of the fixed and growth mindsets. by changing your mindset, you enter a new world. one world is about validation, while the other is about stretching one's self. one takes failure is setbacks, while the other failure is only achieved if you don't grow and not striving for your potential. one world sees effort as a failing, while the other see it as the virtue. you have a choice, as mindsets are just what you believe, and you can change what you believe. so choose which will get you to where you want to be.
### Is Success About Learning—Or Proving You’re Smart?
Benjamin Barber, an eminent political theorist, once said, “I don’t divide the world into the weak and the strong, or the successes and the failures. . . . I divide the world into the learners and nonlearners.”

What makes someone a nonlearner? from a young age we all learn to walk and talk with no real fears of failure. but for some that seemingly ends at some point. its ended by the fixed mindset. as soon as children find their limits, they become afraid of challenges that exceed them. studies show that children who develop a fixed mindset when asked if they would rather redo a jigsaw or try another harder one will stick to the safer one. "smart kids don't make mistakes". kids with the growth mindset feel otherwise.
#### Beyond Puzzles
but this goes beyond just kids and puzzles. students who are not fluent in English at the University of Hong Kong, where the syllabus is served in English, were asked if they would take a course offered by the staff to increase English proficiency. those with a fixed mindset flatly expressed disinterest, while growth mindset students jumped at the chance. for a short term feeling of intelligence for not having to face trying to learn a new language, a hard feat, students were willing to put their education and potential future careers at risk. this is nonlearning.
#### Brain Waves Tell the Story
it's even detectable in brainwave scans. fixed mindset subjects when told if they were right or wrong and feedback only focused on the result, but not how they could improve. meanwhile growth mindset subjects paid close attention to the feedback.
#### What’s Your Priority?
but this really goes further than just the intellectual learning aspects. when young adults were asked if they would rather have relationships that make them feel safe or challenge them, individuals with a growth mindset responded with wanting to be put on a pedestal, feeling perfect or worshipped. meanwhile those with the growth mindset seeked a partner that sees their faults but helps them work on them, challenges them to be better and encourages them to learn new things. they didnt want people to be mean to them but they wanted them to compliment fostering their development. but what if two partners had opposing mindsets? A growth-mindset woman tells about her marriage to a fixed-mindset man:

sometimes this stretching can even carry you to something impossible. ## 5.    BUSINESS: MINDSET AND LEADERSHIP

Read Gerstner’s excellent book Who Says Elephants Can’t Dance? to see how it’s done.

Help them fill both roles.

## 7.    PARENTS, TEACHERS, AND COACHES: WHERE DO MINDSETS COME FROM?
the message kids are sent by parents are incredibly important. and it's not the direct messages, but the implicit ones. the message that they will be a fuck up for making mistakes, that they can't draw ugly pictures, that they can't break things even by accident.
### Parents (and Teachers): Messages About Success and Failure
#### Messages About Success
in the end, praise sends the wrong messages to kids about success, that you only succeed if you are consistently correct and never make mistakes. that if you make a mistake you are thus a failure.
##### SENDING MESSAGES ABOUT PROCESS AND GROWTH
but with this, you can praise the *right things*. if you praise effort, you are cementing that the only way they fail is if they don't try or change tactics when things aren't working, which is really the only thing that matters. but this praise needs to be consistent, you can't avoid fixed mindset praise with your kids and then use it on others, as it will cement the same message to your children.
##### REASSURING CHILDREN
in the same vein, simply saying "you're smart" instead of "you've tried and done the work so you have this" sends that same message.
#### Messages About Failure
the general consensus parents take with kids is to in general avoid ever failing. instead, you should acknowledge *why* your child failed. are there others making more effort? who have been working longer? if they really want it, they have to work hard to reach that.
##### CONSTRUCTIVE CRITICISM: MORE ABOUT FAILURE MESSAGES
constructive criticism means giving critcism that helps the child grow, not reprimanding them.
#### Children Learn the Messages
kids with a fixed mindset pick up on how they are getting constant messages of judgment. meanwhile growth mindset kids understood their parents wanted them to learn and be good students, not smart. this is set by the messages they are sent, so nurture growth, not judgements.
##### CHILDREN PASS ON THE MESSAGES
and children pass on this message too, kids with a growth mindset naturally give growth mindseted advice and mirror what they were raised on.
##### ISN’T DISCIPLINE TEACHING?
in reality ""disciplining" a child sets the message that they will be judged and punished if they go against parent's rules and values. this works, but it doesn't promote the ability for children to think things through and come to decisions on their own. treating your kids as equals on some level allows them to develop their own values and beliefs.
##### MINDSETS CAN BE A LIFE-AND-DEATH MATTER
mindsets can have a massive impact on a child's life considering their life is just beginning. but putting pressure on a kid from a fixed mindset just makes them more stressed out.
##### WANTING THE BEST IN THE WORST WAY
sometimes the idea that parents want their kids to go to "the right place" can rob them of opportunities that are mor beneficial. a college may have a good course but a child denied by their parent cause it isn't Harvard.
##### “WE LOVE YOU—ON OUR TERMS”
ultimately the message all this sends is parents love you, as long as you follow their terms.
##### IDEALS
it's important to set the right ideals. not that your kid needs to be perfect, but they need to be their best self and contribute to society.
### Teachers (and Parents): What Makes a Great Teacher (or Parent)?
so what makes a great teacher (and extension, a great parent).
#### Great Teachers
great teachers instil a growth mindset.
The great teachers believe in the growth of the intellect and talent, and they are fascinated with the process of learning. Marva Collin was a great teacher, despite her classes being tough with kids that had been discard, they all said they like it, because it makes their brains bigger. she did this not by saying the kids can do great things, she told them they *will* do great things.

you can set high standards, but you need to create an atmosphere that kids can reach those standards and are already valid, not after they reach it.

but the standards also has to be relative. if the reading rates are 20 points lower than the national average, every point gain matters and is worth celebrating.
#### Hard Work and More Hard Work
in the end teachers also need to guide kids to those standards. they need to encourage them that they are capable and remind them that it's a learning process, where they can reach anything.

students that don't care is a precarious situation. but the correct technique is to discipline them, but by prompting their own learning and that you are heard to help them, but only if they are willing to help themselves.

growth minded teachers are people that want to be directly involved and nurturing children, to help them reach whatever they want to.

#### The Fixed-Mindset Coach in Action
the fixed mindset means coaches can begin to see their players at locked in pieces, even if they don't intend it to. even if they think their players can grow, they may think themselves to be fixed in skill, that any loss is a measurement of their own coaching ability and thus lash out at their team in a fixed mindset.

sometimes no mistakes does occur, but this is more happenstance than the standard. and when mistakes are made, it can do more harm and only cause further mistakes form that harm.

##### A COACH FOR ALL SEASONS
meanwhile a growth mindset lets teams succeed, and consistently succeed, even rising from the bottom.

the growth mindset doesn't ask for mistake free games, but full effort games.

the growth mindset finally isn't just saying "you can do anything". it instils in them they can reach their goals by gaining skills and finding resources to reach those goals. just saying you can do anything doesn't push them anywhere.

## 8.    CHANGING MINDSETS
### The Nature of Change
#### Beliefs Are the Key to Happiness (and to Misery)
beliefs largely govern your mental problems and thus mindset. thoughts of "people think I'm incompetent" or "this isn't working, so it will never work". people aren't conscious of these beliefs, but people can be taught to change these beliefs, this is what cognitive therapy is. even if you aren't aware of it, everyone has a running count in their head, the issue arises when your mental interpretation makes the count go awry, because you then feel exaggerated feelings.
#### Mindsets Go Further
mindsets largely govern how the running count plays out in reality and how it is managed. the fixed mindset turns this counter into a label, that you're good, or you're bad. the growth mindset still has you keeping track of things, but more in the context of how it benefits their learning. cognitive therapy addresses the extreme judgement by making you draw different conclusions, but it doesn't address the root mindset, that attributes are fixed, and thus is more like a band aid than fixing the direct issue.
### The Mindset Lectures
just learning about the growth mindset can make a big difference. it doesn't make it easy to grow, but it gives you courage to embrace your goals and dreams and reaching them.
### More About Change
change seems easy this way, but people that hold on to the fixed mindset often have a reason. it gives them a narrative that gives them a path to self-esteem and respect from others. the idea that you are worthy and loved comes naturally with the fixed mindset. it makes a self that is strong and what people want them to be. the growth mindset asks people to discard this image and instead admit their deficiencies, which is not that easy. the fixed mindset offers you refuge from that scary idea, but you need to resist it. the growth mindset doesn't diminish you, but makes you able to be more than that.
### Opening Yourself Up to Growth
the best way to open yourself up to growth is to retrospect on what steps you can take to growth in certain situations. think about your goal and what steps you can take to keep you on track to head forward. what additional info can you gather to increase your chance of success? you could take more steps to make more diverse attempts and avenues towards it, apply for more jobs or schools. you could improve your applications, and what people are looking for more, and then seek out ways to gain those attributes.
#### Plans That You’ll Carry Out and Ones That You Won’t
if you don't have a concrete plan, you probably won't carry them out. so make concrete plans, plans that include when, where, how you will do it. and make sure it is a growth mindset plan.
#### Feeling Bad, But Doing Good
these plans also can get you out of the shit. you can be miserable, completely dejected, but if you make a concrete, growth-oriented plan, you are sure to feel better if you stick to it. not if you can, because you will stick to it.
#### The Number One Draft Choice
if your goal is to become a professional from an amateur, you need to keep in mind how big of a step that is. it will take adjustment and learning. you should seek out resources to facilitate this, and be vulnerable about your potential fears and issues you might face, as it will allow you to get good feedback on how to better yourself. people want to help you, not hinder you, so use them as a resource for their wisdom. don't worry about if you're overpaid, work hard to become someone worth that salary.
### People Who Don’t Want to Change
#### Entitlement: The World Owes You
many people with a fixed mindset don't think they need to change, but the world does. they think the world owes them something. it's frightening to give up the idea you are superior and owed something, that you are just ordinary run of the mill. you could first consider that effort and commitment is what makes people stand out in life, but you need to understand that sometimes effort doesn't pan out, but the point isn't the success, but the effort allowing you to try again with a better chance of success. you need to enjoy the effort as much as the final product. you need to build relatinships with people that help you and them develop. 
#### Denial: My Life Is Perfect
but it's also hard to admit your life isn't perfect. but you need to think about *how* things came to be not exactly perfect. perhaps you aren't listening to others, and you need to acknowledge why you arent hearing them. perhaps in your fixed mindset, you see descent or requests as criticism which you don't want to hear. its an attack on your character. if you took a growth mindset, you could overcome this fear of judgement 
### Changing Your Child’s Mindset
#### The Precocious Fixed Mindsetter
you can't always control what mindset your child takes, their teachers and people around them can have a massive effect on them that are out of your control. but instead of trying to talk them out of it, you need to live a growth mindset. you need to implement the growth mindset into your everyday life in a way to continuously expose them to it. take interest in their learning and growth, acknowledge their mistakes and encourage them to work hard.
#### Effort Gone Awry
but you need to acknowledge situations where your child is perhaps being harmed by their efforts and pressure. you need to be involved in their learning but also kids are meant to have fun. if they are worried sick that's a wake up call.
if you have high hopes for your kids, you need to acknowledge what they want, and if they are so anxious it's doing harm, they aren't wanting the same thing. temper your expectations and pressures, and acknowledge they can reach their goals with an amount of effort, but it shouldn't be so much effort it kills them. change tactics, go to a diffrent school, change tutors, let them be a kid.
### Mindset and Willpower
willpower is directly connected to mindset, willpower is something you can learn.
####  Anger, The Growth Mindset and Self-Control
people see also anger and temper as something you have or just don't have. as you can guess, this isn't really the case. think about what gets you worked up, and why it does. think about then what reaction you have to it, and how you read the reaction to your reaction, people can't read your mind, so instead of lashing out, perhaps voice *what* is upsetting you. if your loved one is worth anything, they will understand and respond positively. if you can't manage that, you can remove yourself from the situation and find a better method of letting off steam. if you're uptight, work on understanding people don't have malice, and perhaps get a sense of humor about it. people arent good bad, or weak strong, they are just people, so don't assume they are infalliable.

change isn't about picking up some tricks and suddenly it's fixed, its a process you need to work on and upkeep forever. once change is achieved, you need to keep up or you will only change back. it might even get worse as you see things can work and suddenly they arent again. you need to be dedicated to the new mindset, not just try to find some techniques to patch over it.
### The Journey to a (True) Growth Mindset
so how do you get a true growth mindset?
#### The Journey: Step 1
first, acknowledge your fixed mindset. acknowledge you have a fixed mindset in certain situations, since you can't work on a problem if you don't acknowledge it exists.
#### The Journey: Step 2
next, acknowledge what triggers you to enter that fixed mindset. when does your fixed mindset persona appear and make you judge or doubt yourself? don't judge yourself for not being a growth saint, just observe it.
#### The Journey: Step 3
now give that persona a name. this lets you more easily discuss how it shows up, how you felt and acted in that fixed mindset and how it effected others. you drag their behaviour and put it on someone else other than yourself, indirectly using your tendency to judge for your benefit. once done, you can better work on your own triggers and communicate to others your needs to circumvent them.
#### The Journey: Step 4
now that you know your triggers and have named them, take the persona on the journey with you. be prepared when they will show up since now you can predict their appearance, greet it but dismiss it's concerns and ask it to come with you: “Look, I know this may not work out, but I’d really like to take a stab at it. Can I count on you to bear with me?” if you hit a setback, let it run it's course, do it's song and dance and once it's settled, talk about what you are going to do going forward: “Yes, yes, it’s possible that I’m not so good at this (yet), but I think I have an idea of what to do next. Let’s just try it.” when you are under pressure and begin to doubt things, communicate this and ask what is needed to do your best work. calm your fixed mindset persona and cut them some slack so you can contribute.

your fixed mindset persona exists to try to protect you, but it limits you by doing that. so educate it with the growth mindset so it supports you, understand it's point of view but slowly re-educate it. understand everyone has a fixed mindset, so give them sympathy in their struggles.

#### The summary
- It starts by accepting that we all have both mindsets.
- Then we learn to recognize what triggers our fixed mindset. Failures? Criticism? Deadlines? Disagreements?
- And we come to understand what happens to us when our fixed-mindset “persona” is triggered. Who is this persona? What’s its name? What does it make us think, feel, and do? How does it affect those around us?
- Importantly, we can gradually learn to remain in a growth-mindset place despite the triggers, as we educate our persona and invite it to join us on our growth-mindset journey.
- Ideally, we will learn more and more about how we can help others on their journey, too.
### Learn and Help Learn
if you've tamed your fixed persona, you still need to keep at it. every day presents you more opportunity to learn and grow. each morning remind yourself of the mindsets. ask yourself these questions:

- What are the opportunities for learning and growth today? For myself? For the people around me?
- Make a plan to grab these opportunity. when, where, and how will I embark on my plan?
- As you implement the plan, acknowledge your obstacles and setbacks, then ask yourself when, where and how again.
- ask yourself what do I have to do to maintain and continue the growth?
