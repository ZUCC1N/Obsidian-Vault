# Summary
## Chapter 01 
anger is not a reasonable response, it is a response that within it contains no intellectual reason, and is ultimately against nature. nature wishes for peace as humans does, for correction and move to the best state, not malice. anger is like fire, it spreads and spreads until it consumes all. you should not act in anger, but should with reason protect what you have and gain vengeance in time for inflictions.
## Chapter 02
anger despite this, has some aspect of mind, just the irrational part of mind. it arises from the impression you were injured, which is even if not conscious a decision of the mind. it is a passion, and passion is not a chance, but surrendering to a chance prompt, thus anger does have some will involved. acts of anger breath of a perceived injury is no longer anger but now brutality. if the wise man saw every act of lack of virtue and became angry, he would simply go mad. but the layman knows no better, they focus on other things or have aliments that predispose them, so thus you should be calm and firm in hand to correct, not fly to anger. in order to beat back anger, do not fall and when angered, do no wrong. stand from the point of view of those who you may be angered with, act as their ally as if they were there when they are not. anger is born from a weakness of the mind, one that leads you to going to the path of least resistance. no one is faultless, you included, and if you are sinned against you may very well be response for the same sin you afflicted on them. be prepared for anything to occur, and do not punish for sin but take pre-emptive measures to stop further ones.
## Chapter 03
- to cure anger
	- follow a stoic life as you will not perceive injuries
	- take on loads that befit you, lest you grow bitter at things you fail. thus, avoid injury in general. sorround yourself with people befitting this cause,
	- check your passions and what makes you anger and do your best to avoid them.
	- be patient and let reason rule not the whims of anger
