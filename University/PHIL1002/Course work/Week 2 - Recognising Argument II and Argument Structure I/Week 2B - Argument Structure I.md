## Standardizing arguments
1. underline inference indicators
2. identify separate statements and assign a letter and separate with angle brackets
3. put in standard form (no inference indicators)
## Tips and tricks
1. missing inference indicators
	- if they are missing, you can add them to make it easier determine the structure by placing it in square brackets
2. The occurrence of modal words as inference indicators
	- can be difficult identifying due to it being hard spotting modal expressions inside arguments and how they are acting in the argument
3. give disguised statements their own letter
4. disguised statements often involve repetitions, and repetitions receive the same letter
5. Inference indicators are whatever words are doing the job of indicating inferences
6. Inference indicators can be split up
7. Modal words acting as inference indicators should be replaced with non-modal words.
8. Some compound statements can be split up, but some have to remain as a single assertion
	- conditionals cannot be split without affecting its truth/falsity
	- conjunctions can due to their truth not being dependent on each other
	- disjunctions cannot due to their truth being dependent on each other (if one is false the other must be true, separating them breaks this)
9. each item in an argument to which you give a letter in the process of standardization must be a complete assertion in its own right—no more and no less, or can be converted into such an assertion without change in meaning
	- this may involve removing parts, or entirely rewording a statement to add verbs and such
10. Pronouns should be replaced with nouns when you standardize
