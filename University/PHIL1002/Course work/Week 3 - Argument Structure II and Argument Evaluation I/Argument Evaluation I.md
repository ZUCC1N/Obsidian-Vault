## Support and Validity
- truth is a property of statements given premises that a statement is true if the world actually is as it claims to be
- Support it **not** a property of statements, but a **relation between statements** a claimed to exist when we use some statements as a premise and others as conclusion.
- A strong argument will have arguments that support the conclusion.
- When evaluating an argument there are two questions:
	1. Are the premises true?
	2. Where or not they are true, how much support do they offer to the conclusion?
- When evaluating the support of an argument, assume the argument is true.
- Valid = complete **support** for the argument
