Dialectical Materialism is a combination of [[Dialectical Idealism]] and [[Karl Marx|Marx]] and [[Friedrich Engels|Engels]]'s [[Historical Materialism]]. in essence, Dialectical Materialism relies first on an [[Materalism|materialist]] pretense. from this, in combination with Historical Materialism, it can be reasoned that in times of good material conditions ideas can then be better formed. but in a [[Dialectic|dialectical]] sense, good material conditions can also be effected by bad mindsets, philosophy or ideas, creating worse material conditions. this means the materialist view is true, but ideas can directly affect the material world, and the material world directly inform ideas which flow back and effect the material. they are interwoven and connected forces. the difference is the materialist believes that material comes first and trumps ideas, so fighting for material conditions is more important that thinking about it.
