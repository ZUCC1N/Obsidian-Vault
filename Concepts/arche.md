a concept of [[Metaphysics]], meaning a literal sense a word ‘principle’ from greek, or ideally the substance that constitutes all of existence. [[Aristotle]] in *metaphysics* put it as:
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
that of which all existing things are composed and that from which they originally come to be and that into which they finally perish … this they state is the element and principle of the things that are.<br>
Aristotle (384-322 BCE)
</i></p>
much of early Greek philosophy is focused around deriving an arche, most notable rationalistic philosophers having some different element to propose. [[Thales]] proposes water, due to it being everywhere and being able to exist in all 3 physical states. [[Anaximander]] stated the arche to be infinity itself, as all things must intrinsically belong to it but it also belong to them. [[Anaximenes]] stands between stating that the arche is infinite, but primarily moist air itself in different forms.

in reality, quarks and subatomic particles are the true arche, but this was only really founded in the 1950s and 60s, making it fairly recent. this means for most of history philosophy was as close to discovering a scientific arche as science was (if you count saying protons neurons and electrons being elementary incorrect, even if 1 of them is and the other 2 extremely close to being).
