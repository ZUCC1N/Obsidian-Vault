## Biography

Anaximander was a student of [[Thales]] reportedly, but was much more out there than him. He is credited with being the first person to draw a map of the known world and predicted an earthquake. Anaximander was said by Eusebius to have developed gnomons for identifying ‘solstices, timespans, hours horai and equinoxes’. Anaximander thought that humans came originally from fish, and so abstained from eating them on grounds of being related to them. He said that the sun is pure fire and larger than the earth, moonlight being a reflection of the sun's light. he also stated rain was caused by precipitation of water vapor, he stated the earth was a cylinder (but also Diogenes Laertius disagrees saying he said it was a sphere) and floats in the infinite, having no reason to move.

but like Thales, Anaximander’s most distinctive thesis concerns the [[arche]]. He said that the apeiron, ‘the infinite’ or ‘indefinite’, is that from which everything comes into being and returns to, as it is destined to keep balance. A surviving quote of Anaximander reads: 
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
‘where things have their origin there too their passing away occurs according to necessity; they pay justice and reparation to one another for their injustice in conformity with the ordinance of time.’ <br>
Anaximander (610-546 BCE)
</i></p>
he believed the world existed in an equilibrium between the elements, and would naturally balance them in order to keep unity. [[Plutarch]] says in reference to Anaximander:
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
‘the infinite is the universal cause of the generation and destruction of the universe. From it the heavens were separated off and in general all the worlds, infinite in number. He asserted that destruction, and, much earlier, generation occur from time immemorial, all the same things being renewed.’
 <br>
Plutarch (610-546 BCE)
</i></p>
[[Aristotle]] suggests in *Physics* that Anaximander held this belief as the infinite can have no other purpose than to be a principle, and can itself have no principle – that is, cannot derive from anything more fundamental than itself, for if it did it would not be a principle: 
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
‘the region outside the heavens is infinite, then body and worlds also seem to be infinite, for why should there be “here” rather than “there” in the void? If body is anywhere, then it is everywhere. Again, if void and space are infinite, body too must be infinite – for with eternal things there is no difference between being possible and being actual... generation and destruction will come to an end unless there is something infinite from which what comes into being is subtracted.’<br>
Aristotle (384-322 BCE)
</i></p>
