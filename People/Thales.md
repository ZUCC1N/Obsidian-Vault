## Biography
Thales was a native of Miletus, in Anatolia, a flourishing commercial city, in which there was a large slave population, and a class struggle between the rich and poor. Similar conditions prevailed in most of the cities of Anatolia at the time of Thales. like other cities of Ionia, Miletus saw important developments during the seventh and sixth centuries BCE. political power belonged to land owners, but this was gradually replaced by a plutocracy of merchants. They, were replaced by a tyrant, who achieved power by the support of the democratic party. 

As to putting a date to Thales, the best evidence is that he predicted an eclipse which must have taken place in 585 BCE. Thales is said to have travelled in Egypt, and bringing to the Greeks geometry. He seems to have discovered how to calculate the distance of a ship at sea from observations taken at two points on land, and how to estimate the height of a pyramid from the length of its shadow. [[Plato]] recounts in in *Theaetetus* Thales fell into a well because he was gazing up at the stars, not looking where he was going. a story also tells Thales was hired by the ruler of Lydia, King Croesus, to find a way for his army to cross the River Halys without building a bridge. He did it by having the army camp on the bank, dig a ditch to divert the river around the camp, so it would be shallow enough to cross. [[Aristotle]] says in *Politics*:
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
'He was reproached for his poverty, which was supposed to show that philosophy is of no use. According to the story, he knew by his skill in the stars while it was yet winter that there would be a great harvest of olives in the coming year; so, having a little money, he gave deposits for the use of all the olive-presses in Chios and Miletus, which he hired at a low price because no one bid against him. When the harvest time came, and many were wanted all at once and of a sudden, he let them out at any rate which he pleased, and made a quantity of money. Thus he showed the world that philosophers can easily be rich if they like, but that their ambition is of another sort.<br>
- Aristotle (384-322 BCE)
</i></p>
Thales followed a primary topic; the origin of the world, reality and its workings. Thales’ [[arche]] was water. Water is everywhere – the sea, it falls from the sky, we are made of it, if you cut a plant you find it, soil is damp, animals die without it. also, to the mind at the time, water is the only substance that can be a solid, liquid and gas interchangeably. this means it is a very sound and logical candidate to be the arche.

Aristotle also tells us in *On the Soul* that he believes Thales thought the soul caused movement, for he is said to have stated the soul of magnets move iron, and such that, 
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
‘soul is mixed in with the whole universe, and perhaps this is why Thales supposed that all things are full of gods.’ <br>
Aristotle (384-322 BCE)
