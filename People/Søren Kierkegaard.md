## Biography
Kierkegaard is widely regarded to be one of the first existentialists, laying the ground work for everyone that came after him. sources otherwise tend to ignore him, since he presents an issue in how he follows a more Kantian school of thought but was alive around when Schopenhauer was writing. He was critical on organized religion, specifically the Lutheran church of Denmark, and topics of morality and ethics. He was mainly concerned with how one lives as a  "single individual", valuing human reality over abstract thinking and highlighting one's self-determination. 

Opposite [[Jean-Paul Sartre]] and the atheistic existentialism paradigm, Kierkegaard focused on Christian existentialism. While scientists learn about the world by observation, Kierkegaard emphatically denied that observation alone could reveal the inner workings of the world of the spirit.
