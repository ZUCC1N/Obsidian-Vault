## Biography
Anaxagoras was an Ionian, and carried on the scientific, rationalist tradition of the region. He was the first to suggest mind as the primary cause of physical changes. Plato, in the [[Phaedrus]], says:
<p style="text-align: center;"><i>
'[Pericles] fell in, it seems with Anaxagoras, who was a scientific man; and satiating himself with the theory of things on high, and having attained to a knowledge of the true nature of intellect and folly, which were just what the discourses of Anaxagoras were mainly about, he drew from that source whatever was of a nature to further him in the art of speech.'<br>
- Plato (428-348 BCE)
</i></p>
Anaxagoras was initially met with hostility in his attempt to introduce higher education, leading to when Pericles' opponents began a campaign against him, Anaxagoras fell under fire. They passed a law allowing for the impeachment of atheists and who taught theories about 'the things on high'. Under this law, they prosecuted Anaxagoras, who was accused of teaching that the sun was a red-hot stone and the moon was earth. the specifics aren't certain, but this lead to Anaxagoras being made to leave Athens most likely with the assistance of Pericles. He returned to Ionia, where he founded a school.

Anaxagoras held that everything is infinitely divisible,  and accepted Parmenides’ view that what ultimately exists must be conserved, and that what is seen as creation and destruction is mixings and separations of eternal elements. What originally exists before the worlds come into existence was a mixture of the seeds of things, each of them itself composed of infinitely small parts. In addition there is ‘mind’ which acts upon things and puts them in motion and collects mass into things, and their demise separate them. There is no void, the universe is everything there is, and the void is simply air.
